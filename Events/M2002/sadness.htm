Sadness at the close of the Athletes Village


The Games banners are carried away
FOR two glorious weeks it was home to over 5,000 athletes and officials from across the world. Today, apart from the cleaners, the athletes' village at Fallowfield was deserted.

With the last of the athletes bidding farewell yesterday, their rooms are being turned back over to students of Manchester University.

They were waved off with some sadness by volunteers. Crossing patrol warden Susan Lockton, 57, from Droylsden, said: "I'm really sorry to see all the athletes go, I've had such a fantastic time."

Amanda Dale, a psychiatric nurse from Knutsford, worked in the athletes' telephone section of the village.  She said: "It's a bit like a bereavement now that everyone has gone, it's very sad.

"Most of the athletes were friendly, but the Canadians were the best, they had a wonderful attitude." Eddie McCann, Charlotte Postlethwaite and Barbara Sidebotham worked as day pass centre assistants.

Eddie said: " We've had a great time working here and we even met the Queen."

Charlotte said: "Being part of the Commonwealth Games has been a once in a lifetime experience and it's something I'll never forget."

Barbara said: "I will certainly miss being here because it has been a great atmosphere."

Stephen Peel, who ran the hairdressing salon in the village, said: "It has been superb meeting all the athletes from different countries. It's something I will always remember. The event has done Manchester proud."
