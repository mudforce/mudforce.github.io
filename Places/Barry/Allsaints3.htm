All Saints Church, Barry

Completion of All Saints Part 2

By 1914, the Church Building Committee were able to proceed with the construction of the second part of the church. Work on the Chancel, Tower and Vestries started in April of that year, the corner stone being laid on 3 June 1914 by Lady Beatrice Stewart. Shortly before this, at the Easter Vestry Meeting on 16 April, Rector Stewart announced his intention of relinquishing his incumbency of Porthkerry with Barry in favour of his young colleague, the Rev. T.W. Welsh.

By 4 August, when war was declared against Germany, the walls of the Chancel, Tower and Vestries had reached a height of about six feet. Some memebers of the Building Committee advocated the suspension of the work until the war was over, but the Rev. T.W. Welch was opposed, being anxious to see the church completed. He had his way and the building work continued.

The completed Church was consecrated and the Organ and Lectern dedicated by the Lord Bishop of Llandaff on Saturday, 24 April 1915. The completed church, with seating accommodation for 573 worshippers, cost £10,870.
